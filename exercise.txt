won = to give
mako ngun-won wolehwole - she's giving the gun to me this afternoon
ngudda ngan-wong wolehwole - you gave it to me yesterday evening

yikan = to fetch
ngaye nga-bo-yikan - I'm fetching water
ngaye nga-bo-yikang - I fetched water

or just kan:

nga-bo-kan - I collected water

birrhme = to clean up
karri-bolk-birrhme
birri-bolk-birrhmeng

wokngimen = to telephone
ngaye wokngimen wolehwole
kandi-wokngimeng

makmen = to get better (from ka-mak) (kun-njilng = feelings)
nga-njilng-makmen
yi-njilng-makmen bolkkime?
